বাংলাদেশ

No country on Earth is shaped as completely by rivers. The Ganges, Brahmaputra and Meghna converge here, in the largest river delta in the world, before surrendering to the Bay of Bengal — carrying silt that has built the land itself, harvest after harvest, for millennia.

World Heritage

বিশ্ব ঐতিহ্য

Inscribed 1997

সুন্দরবন

The largest contiguous mangrove forest on Earth, spanning the delta where the Ganges meets the sea — and the last stronghold of the wild Royal Bengal Tiger.

Inscribed 1985

মসজিদের শহর বাগেরহাট

A 15th-century Sultanate capital of brick mosques and mausoleums, built by the saint-general Khan Jahan Ali in the Sundarbans' shadow.

Inscribed 1985

পাহাড়পুর বৌদ্ধ বিহার

Somapura Mahavihara — the largest Buddhist monastery south of the Himalayas, a scholarly beacon of the Pala Empire.

History, Chosen Moments

ইতিহাসের পথ

1952

ভাষা আন্দোলন

Students in Dhaka are killed defending Bengali as a state language — the Language Movement becomes the moral root of a future nation, and its date, February 21, is now the UN's International Mother Language Day.

1971

মুক্তিযুদ্ধ

A nine-month Liberation War, fought against immense odds, ends on 16 December 1971 with the birth of an independent Bangladesh.

1972

নতুন সংবিধান

Bangladesh adopts its constitution, founded on nationalism, secularism, democracy and socialism.

1999

একুশে ফেব্রুয়ারি

UNESCO declares 21 February International Mother Language Day, carrying Bangladesh's 1952 sacrifice to the entire world.

Today

চলমান গল্প

A young, fast-growing nation of writers, garment workers, farmers and engineers — still being written, river by river.
